Deadliest Battles of The World Wars

the top 6 most deadliest battles that were fought during World War 1 and World War 2



Battle of Passchendaele

The batte of Passchendaele was fought between the allies and the German empire during the first world war. It was the battle which symbolized the slaughter and the grittiness of the war. More than 495k lives were lost and very little progress was made

Battle of the Somme

The Battle of the Somme was fought by the British Empire and the French Republic against the German Empire in 1916. The Somme offensive was launched in order to relieve the French and weaken the German army.

Battle of Verdun

The battle of Verdun was a battle fought between the Germans and the French. This was the longest and the most bloodiest battle the French and the Germans have fought. Around 300,000 lives were lost in this conflict.

Operation Overlord

The invasion of the Normandy beaches involved the Americans, the British, and the Canadians going against the German defenses. The invasion begin on June 6th 1944; the allies achieved victory but had to pay a heavy price of casualties.

Battle of Stalingrad

The battle of Stalingrad is considered the bloodiest battle of WW2 and also a major turning point. The Germans lost this batte because of the climate, Russian superiority of quantities in taks, equipment, and soldiers; but the main reason the Germans lost was because of Hitler's poor decisions of leading the German army. The Russians caused the Germans to go into a full retreat.

Battle of Berlin

The Battle of Berlin was the last battle of WW2 that was fought in the European theatre. The battle was the final stand of Germany going against the soviets. The Germans were outnumbered in every single way that their civillians had to bear arms and fight the Soviet Soldiers.
